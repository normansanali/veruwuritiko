Josh Giddey Age, Birthday, Height, Weight, Girlfriend, Parents, Net Worth, BIO &amp; Stats

Josh Giddey (born 10 October 2002, Age: 21 years old) is a young and talented Australian basketball player who currently plays for the Oklahoma City Thunder in the NBA. He was selected by the Thunder with the sixth overall pick in the 2021 NBA draft, becoming the highest-drafted Australian player in NBA history.

Giddey is also the youngest player ever to record a triple-double in the NBA, achieving the feat at 19 years and 84 days old. He has quickly established himself as one of the most promising and versatile players in the league, averaging 14.7 points, 7.7 rebounds, and 6.2 assists per game in his second season.

In this article, we will explore Josh Giddey’s age, birthday, height, weight, girlfriend, parents, net worth, bio, and stats, and learn more about his remarkable journey from Melbourne to Oklahoma City.

Early Life and Education

Joshua James Giddey was born on October 10, 2002, in Melbourne, Victoria, Australia. He grew up in a basketball-loving family, as both his parents, Warrick and Kim, played professionally for the Melbourne Tigers in the Australian leagues. His older sister, Hannah, also plays basketball and is currently a forward for the Oral Roberts Golden Eagles in the NCAA Division I.

Josh attended St Kevin’s College in Melbourne from Years 7 to 10, where he played basketball under the guidance of his father, who was the head coach. He then moved to Canberra to join the NBA Global Academy, a training center at the Australian Institute of Sport (AIS) that prepares young prospects for the NBA. He also attended UC Senior Secondary College Lake Ginninderra alongside his full-time training at the AIS.

Early Career

Giddey showed his potential as a leader and a playmaker at various tournaments and camps, both nationally and internationally. He led VIC Metro to the title at the Australian Under-18 Championships in April 2019, averaging 20 points, 8.3 rebounds, and six assists per game. He also helped the NBA Global Academy win the Torneo Junior Ciutat de L’Hospitalet in Barcelona in January 2020, where he earned the Most Valuable Player (MVP) honors. He participated in the Basketball Without Borders camp at the NBA All-Star Weekend in Chicago in February 2020, where he was named an all-star.

Adelaide 36ers (2020–2021)

On March 12, 2020, Giddey signed with the Adelaide 36ers of the National Basketball League (NBL) as part of the league’s Next Stars program, which aims to develop NBA draft prospects. He became the first Australian player to join the program, turning down offers from several NCAA Division I programs, including Arizona.

Giddey made his NBL debut on January 15, 2021, recording eight points, four rebounds, and three assists in a 94–91 loss to the Brisbane Bullets. He gradually improved his game and became a regular starter for the 36ers, showcasing his vision, passing, rebounding, and scoring abilities. He also made history by becoming the youngest Australian in NBL history to record a triple-double, with 12 points, 10 rebounds, and 10 assists in a 93–77 loss to the New Zealand Breakers on April 26, 2021.

He followed that up with another triple-double in his next game, with 15 points, 13 assists, and 11 rebounds in a 101–79 win over the Brisbane Bullets on May 1, 2021, becoming the first Australian ever to record back-to-back triple-doubles in the NBL.

Giddey finished his rookie season with the 36ers averaging 10.9 points, 7.4 rebounds, 7.5 assists, and 1.1 steals per game in 28 games. He was named the NBL Rookie of the Year and was also selected to the All-NBL Second Team. He declared for the 2021 NBA draft on April 27, 2021, and was projected as a first-round pick by many analysts and scouts.

Oklahoma City Thunder (2021–present)

On July 29, 2021, Giddey was drafted by the Oklahoma City Thunder with the sixth overall pick in the 2021 NBA draft, becoming the highest-drafted Australian player ever, surpassing Ben Simmons, who was selected with the first overall pick in 2016. He signed his rookie contract with the Thunder on August 8, 2021, but suffered a left ankle sprain in his NBA Summer League debut later that day, which ruled him out of the rest of the competition.

Giddey made his NBA debut on October 20, 2021, recording four points, 10 rebounds, and three assists in a 107–86 loss to the Utah Jazz. He scored his first NBA points on a three-pointer in the first quarter. He recorded his first double-double on October 24, 2021, with 18 points and 10 assists in a 115–103 win over the Philadelphia 76ers. He became the youngest player in NBA history to record a triple-double on November 10, 2021, with 17 points, 13 rebounds, and 14 assists in a 123–115 win over the New Orleans Pelicans.

He was 19 years and 84 days old at the time, breaking the previous record held by LaMelo Ball, who was 19 years and 140 days old when he achieved the feat. Giddey also became the first player since Oscar Robertson in 1961 to record three consecutive triple-doubles as a rookie, with 19 points, 11 rebounds, and 10 assists in a 128–109 win over the Golden State Warriors on November 18, 2021, and 22 points, 12 rebounds, and 12 assists in a 134–91 win over the Portland Trail Blazers on November 20, 2021.

As of November 24, 2021, Giddey is averaging 14.7 points, 7.7 rebounds, and 6.2 assists per game in his second season with the Thunder. He ranks second among all rookies in points, first in rebounds, first in assists, and fourth in steals per game. He is also shooting 45.7 percent from the field, 29.1 percent from three-point range, and 72.8 percent from the free-throw line. He has been praised by his teammates, coaches, opponents, and analysts for his maturity, basketball IQ, creativity, and versatility on the court.
