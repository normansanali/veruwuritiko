Career

Song Kang-ho started his career as a theatre artist and joined Kee Kuk-SEO’s theatre company. In the year 1991, he made his first stage appearance with a play titled A Little Monk. After that, he has played many famous roles in popular plays for nearly a decade. In the year 1996, he made his filming debut with his appearance in the movie titled The Day a Pig Fell into the Well.

After that, he played some notable roles in popular South Korean movies like Bad Movie, No. 3, Shiri, The Foul King, Joint Security Area, Sympathy for Mr. Vengeance, YMCA Baseball Team, and several others after which he rose to national prominence.

In the year 2003, he made his appearance in a thriller movie titled Memories of Murder. In the very next year, he has worked in the film titled The President’s Barber based on the real-life of the South Korean President’s barber.
